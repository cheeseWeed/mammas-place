Unit 1

Getting Your License

Utah Driver Handbook -- the admin stuff before you drive.

About 20 minutes. ~24 slides.

What this unit teaches

  • How old you have to be for each step
  • What papers to bring to the DLD
  • What the letters on a license actually mean
  • When your license expires and how to renew

By the end you will know exactly what you need to do, in order, to get yourself behind the wheel.

MUST KNOW

Can I get a permit at 15?

Yes -- at 15.

The Entry-Level Learner Permit is the very first card you carry. You can apply the day you turn 15.

  • It is good for 18 months
  • You drive only with a qualifying adult in the front seat
  • Fee: $19
📖 Handbook p.17, p.19
MUST KNOW

What do I bring to the DLD?

  • Original or certified copies only. No photocopies, no faxes.
  • Proof of identity (birth certificate, passport)
  • Proof of Social Security number
  • Two proofs of Utah residence -- each dated within 90 days
  • Fee

Show up with photocopies and you go home without a permit.

📖 Handbook p.23, p.25

What does "Class D" mean?

Class D = your normal license.

Covers personal vehicles up to 26,000 lbs -- cars, SUVs, pickups, most RVs.

Quick check

A Class D license covers personal vehicles up to what weight?

Answer: 26,000 lbs
Class D is the regular driver license -- covers personal vehicles up to 26,000 lbs. Above that, you need a CDL class.

Quick check

What is the minimum age to apply for a Utah Class D driver license?

Answer: 16
Minimum age for a Utah Class D driver license is 16. The learner permit is available at 15.
MUST KNOW

M = motorcycle

M

The single letter M on the back of a Utah license means "can ride a motorcycle." This one shows up on the real DLD test.

Other common ones: H = hazmat, P = passenger, S = school bus, T = doubles/triples, X = hazmat + tanker, Z = taxicab. Adult stuff.

📖 Handbook p.22, p.90
MUST KNOW

Restriction B = corrective lenses

B

If you needed glasses or contacts to pass the vision test, your license carries restriction B. That means: drive with them on, every time.

A = no restrictions. G = daylight hours only.

MUST KNOW

When does my license expire?

TypeLasts
Provisional (under 21)5 years
Regular (21+)8 years

Both expire on your birthday. You can renew up to 6 months before the expiration date.

Move? Tell the DLD within 10 days.

You want to legally ride a motorcycle in Utah. What endorsement do you need on your license?

Answer: M
The M endorsement is required to operate a motorcycle. Without it, you can be fined.

MUST KNOW

The GDL ladder

Utah Graduated Driver License goes in steps:

  1. 1. Entry-Level Permit -- age 15. Adult 21+ in front seat. 18 months max.
  2. 2. Renewal Permit (if needed) -- age 16. After 6 months on permit + driver ed for 16-17 year-olds.
  3. 3. Provisional Class D License -- age 16. Pass skills test. Drive solo with curfew + passenger rules.
  4. 4. Regular Class D License -- automatic at 21. Restrictions drop off.

The "6-month hold" trips people up: under 18 must hold the permit 6 months AND finish driver ed before getting licensed.

📖 Handbook p.14, p.16, p.17

Quick check

What is the minimum age to apply for a Utah entry-level learner permit?

Answer: 15
An entry-level learner permit can be obtained at age 15. A full Class D license requires age 16.
MUST KNOW

What changes at 16?

You can take the skills test at the DLD and get your provisional license -- but two restrictions kick in:

  • Curfew: no driving 12:00 a.m. - 5:00 a.m.
  • Passengers: immediate family members only, for the first 6 months

Curfew exceptions: licensed 21+ adult riding along, driving to/from work, to/from a school-sponsored activity, agricultural work, or emergency.

📖 Handbook p.18
SHOULD KNOW

What changes at 21?

  • "Provisional" comes off your license -- it becomes a regular Class D
  • License expiration jumps from 5 years to 8 years
  • Curfew and passenger rules end (they ended at 17, actually)
  • Phone rule relaxes: voice/hands-free only while moving

At 17, both the curfew and family-only rules drop off automatically.

📖 Handbook p.18, p.20, p.28
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: just got the permit -- drive solo?

Sarah is 15 and just got her permit yesterday. She wants to drive the half-mile to a friend house alone.
No -- ever. A learner permit requires a parent, guardian, instructor, or licensed adult 21+ (with parent authorization) in the front seat. Distance does not matter. Permit hold time does not matter.
📖 Handbook p.17, p.18

Quick check

A 16-year-old licensed Utah driver may NOT drive during which hours?

Answer: 12 a.m. to 5 a.m.
Age 16 license holders have a 12 a.m. to 5 a.m. curfew, with limited exceptions (school activity, work, emergency, or accompanied by a licensed 21+ adult).
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: friend needs a ride at 11:45 p.m.

Marcus, 16, got his provisional license 2 months ago. It is 11:45 p.m. He wants to pick up a friend from a school dance that ran long.
Two rules block him. (1) After midnight, curfew hits -- the school-activity exception only covers driving to and from his own event, not a pickup. (2) Even before midnight, the first-6-months passenger rule allows immediate family only.
📖 Handbook p.18
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: family moves to Utah from California

Dad (43) has a valid California license. Daughter (17) has a California learner permit.
Dad: yes -- for 6 months. Out-of-state licenses are good in Utah up to 6 months; then he must trade it for a Utah DL.
Daughter: no. Out-of-state learner permits do not transfer -- she cannot drive in Utah at all on it.
📖 Handbook p.14, p.28

Quick check

To drive on a Utah public highway, you must have what?

Answer: A valid Utah driver license, learner permit, or temporary driver license
Every Utah resident driving on public highways must have a valid Utah driver license, learner permit, or temporary driver license.
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: 19 and never had a license

Diego is 19 and wants his first license. Does he need driver education?
No -- at 19+ he can skip driver ed if he (1) holds the learner permit at least 90 days AND (2) completes 40 hours of practice driving (10 at night). Driver ed is an option, not a requirement, once you are 19.
📖 Handbook p.16, p.17
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: passed at a third-party tester -- done?

Avery, 15, signs up at a third-party testing service and passes. She thinks she has her license.
Not done. Third-party testers handle the skills test only -- they do not do the written knowledge test. Avery still owes: written test at the DLD (80%), Traffic Safety & Trends online (100%), 6-month permit hold, and driver ed.
📖 Handbook p.33, p.35

Quick check

A newly licensed 16-year-old in Utah may carry which passengers during the first 6 months?

Answer: Immediate family members only
For the first 6 months of having a Utah license at age 16, only immediate family members may be in the vehicle (with limited exceptions).
MUST KNOWscenario

Scenario: showed up to the skills test

What do you actually bring on skills-test day?
Your own vehicle (safe and registered) + valid registration + valid insurance + a licensed adult (21+, or parent/guardian/spouse 18+) to ride along. Only you and the examiner are in the car during the test. Appointments only.
📖 Handbook p.33, p.34
WEIRD UTAH

The license that is not an ID

Utah has a thing called a Driving Privilege Card (DPC) -- it lets someone legally drive, but it is not valid ID and not valid age proof. You cannot buy alcohol with it, cannot board a plane with it, cannot use it to verify you are 18.

And: it expires every single birthday. Most licenses last 5 or 8 years. This one resets annually.

Another weird one: Utah will not let you hold a driver license AND a state ID card at the same time. Pick one.

📖 Handbook p.20, p.29
RECAP

Top 5 MUSTs from this unit

1. Permit at 15, license at 16. Under 18 holds the permit 6 months + driver ed.
2. Written test 80%. Traffic Safety & Trends 100% online. Bring your own car to skills.
3. Class D = regular license. M = motorcycle. B = corrective lenses.
4. Provisional license = 5 years. Regular (21+) = 8 years. Renew within 6 months. Move? Update within 10 days.
5. At 16: midnight-5 a.m. curfew + family-only passengers for 6 months.
