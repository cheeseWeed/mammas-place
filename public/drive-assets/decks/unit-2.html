Unit 2: Driving Basics

How to actually operate a car — confidently.

~20 minutes · 28 slides · Utah Driver Handbook REV 3.2026 pp. 37-49

Unit 1 covered the paperwork. This unit is what your hands and eyes actually do.

What you'll walk away with

  • A repeatable pre-drive setup so the car fits you
  • Three-step lane changes that feel automatic
  • The parking-distance numbers (15 / 20 / 30 / 50 ft)
  • Hill parking that won't roll away
  • Freeway moves: following, merging, passing

If a slide has a yellow MUST tag, expect it on the test.

SHOULD KNOW

Sit down before you start the car

Back against the seat. Feet flat on the floor. 10 inches of space between your chest and the steering wheel.

  • Top of the wheel: no higher than your shoulders, below your chin
  • Headrest touches the back of your head above your ears (not your neck)

Why? If the air bag fires, you want chest distance and a headrest that catches your head, not your neck. Handbook p. 37.

NICE TO KNOW

Aim mirrors so you can see past your car

  • Left mirror: rest your head against the closed left window, set the mirror so you can barely see the rear left edge of your car
  • Right mirror: lean right until your head is under the rearview mirror, set so you can barely see the right side of your car

Done right, your blind spots almost disappear.

Handbook p. 37 · zerofatalities.com

Quick check

For proper driving position, how much space should be between your chest and the steering wheel?

Answer: 10 inches
Sit with at least 10 inches between your chest and the steering wheel. Protects you from air bag injury. Handbook p. 37.
SHOULD KNOW

Air bags work with your seat belt, not instead of it

  • Air bags deploy at up to 200 mph — that's why distance matters
  • Sit as far back from the air bag as is practical
  • Infants never ride in the front seat with a passenger-side air bag
  • Disabling/removing the air bag system = Class B misdemeanor

The belt holds you in position so the air bag can do its job. Handbook p. 39.

MUST KNOW

Everyone in the car gets a seat belt

Every person in your vehicle must have a fastened seat belt or be in a child seat. No exceptions.

  • Kids 12 and under ride in the back seat
  • Lap belt across hips (not stomach). Shoulder belt across chest (not under arm, not behind back)

Handbook p. 37-38. This is a DLD sample-test canonical fact.  |  Zero Fatalities

Quick check

Air bags are designed to:

Answer: Work WITH safety belts, not as a replacement
Air bags work WITH safety belts - not as a replacement. You still need to be buckled in. Handbook p. 39.
SHOULD KNOW

The 3-step lane change

  1. Mirrors — check rearview and side
  2. Signal — at least 2 seconds before you move
  3. Shoulder check — look over your shoulder for the blind spot, then merge

Same three steps every time. Mirror, signal, shoulder check.

Now answer:

In the 3-step lane change, what is the LAST thing you do right before you steer into the new lane?

Answer: Shoulder check for the blind spot
Mirror, signal, shoulder check — in that order. Mirrors don't show the blind spot, so the over-the-shoulder glance is the LAST step before you move.
SHOULD KNOW

When backing up, look out the back window

Mirrors don't show you what's directly behind the car. Backup cameras can miss low or fast-moving things (kids, bikes).

  • Walk behind the car before getting in
  • Right arm on the seat back, turn and look through the rear window
  • Steer in the direction you want the rear of the car to move
  • Never back up on a freeway or interstate

Quick check

Yellow pavement lines separate traffic moving in which direction?

Answer: Opposing (opposite) directions
YELLOW = OPPOSING. Yellow lines separate traffic going in opposite directions. (White = same direction.) This is the DLD's most-flagged pavement fact.
MUST KNOW

Use the zipper merge — both lanes, then alternate

When two lanes merge into one (construction, ramp), use both lanes all the way to the merge point, then take turns one-by-one into the single lane.

Drivers who merge early actually make the line longer. Zipper is the law in Utah — refusing to zipper is an infraction.

Handbook p. 41. This is a DLD sample-test canonical fact.  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD KNOW

The gore area is the painted triangle — stay out

That triangular wedge between two solid white lines where ramps meet the freeway? That's the gore area.

It's illegal to drive on, cross, or park in. If you missed your exit, take the next one — don't cut back across the gore.

Now answer:

When you parallel park, how close should your back wheel be to the curb?

Answer: Within 12 inches
The back wheel must be no more than 12 inches from the curb. Handbook p. 42.
SHOULD KNOW

Hill parking: three scenarios

SituationWheels turn
Downhill, curb presentToward the curb
Uphill, curb presentAway from the curb
Uphill, no curbTo the right (toward shoulder)

Front tire should touch the curb. Then: parking brake, motor off, lock doors.

Logic: if the brake fails, the curb (or shoulder) catches you instead of traffic. Handbook p. 42.

SHOULD KNOW

Passing: leave 200 ft before you cut back in

  • Left lane is for passing, not cruising
  • When returning to your lane, be at least 200 ft ahead of oncoming traffic
  • Never pass on the shoulder
  • Never pass: at intersections/crosswalks, before a railroad crossing or bridge, on a curve or hill that blocks your view, in a no-passing zone

When passing on a 2-lane road, how far ahead of oncoming traffic must you be before you return to your lane?

Answer: 200 feet

Quick check (3 questions)

You must not park within how many feet of a fire hydrant?

Answer: 15 feet
No parking within 15 feet of a fire hydrant. Handbook p. 42.

You must not park within how many feet of a stop sign, yield sign, traffic signal, or flashing beacon?

Answer: 30 feet
No parking within 30 feet of a flashing beacon, stop sign, yield sign, or traffic signal. Handbook p. 42.

You must not park within how many feet of a crosswalk at an intersection?

Answer: 20 feet
No parking on a crosswalk or within 20 feet of one. Handbook p. 42.
SHOULD KNOW

Snowplow with flashing yellow lights? Don't pass on the blade side.

A working snowplow's blade is sticking out — usually to the right. Passing on that side puts your car against a steel plow blade.

  • Don't pass on whichever side the blade is deployed
  • If you see 3 or more snowplows in a staggered line (echelon), don't pass on either side

Handbook p. 44. Common Utah winter situation.  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD KNOW

Signal at least 2 seconds before you turn or change lanes

Same number for both: 2 seconds minimum lead time.

Signaling as you start the maneuver is too late — other drivers need that warning to react.

Now answer:

How early must you signal before a turn or lane change?

Answer: At least 2 seconds before
Signal at least 2 seconds before any turn or lane change — that's the minimum warning other drivers need to react. Handbook p. 44.
SHOULD KNOW

U-turns need 500 ft of clear sight in both directions

Never U-turn on a curve or near the top of a hill if you can't see 500 feet each way.

Also never U-turn:

  • Where a sign prohibits it
  • On a railroad track or grade crossing
  • On a freeway

Quick check

White pavement lines separate traffic moving in which direction?

Answer: The same direction
WHITE = SAME direction. White lines separate lanes of traffic that flow the same way.
SHOULD KNOW

On-ramps yield. Flex lanes are green-arrow / red-X.

  • On-ramps: you yield to drivers already on the interstate — match their speed and find the gap
  • Flex lanes (rush hour, double-dashed yellow on both sides): green arrow = open, red X = closed
  • Never use an entrance ramp to exit the freeway — illegal, even in a jam

Now answer:

On a Utah flex lane, what does a green ARROW overhead mean, and what does a red X mean?

Answer: Green arrow = open in your direction; red X = lane is closed
Flex lanes reverse direction by time of day. The overhead signal tells you whether the lane is open for your direction. Handbook p. 49.
MUST KNOW

The 2-second rule: how far back you sit

On the freeway, keep at least 2 seconds of following distance behind the car ahead.

How to measure: when the car ahead passes a landmark (sign, shadow line), count "one-thousand-one, one-thousand-two." If you reach the landmark first, you're too close.

Now answer:

You're on an on-ramp to I-15 and traffic on the interstate is heavy with no clear gap. What do you do?

Answer: Use the acceleration lane to match speed, then merge at a gap — interstate has priority
On-ramp traffic yields to interstate traffic. Accelerate to match speed and merge smoothly. Handbook p. 60.
MUST KNOW

Rain, fog, or snow? Stretch it out.

2 seconds is the minimum in good weather. Under adverse conditions, increase it.

ConditionTarget gap
Dry, clear2 seconds
Rain4 seconds
Snow / heavy fog6+ seconds

And: slow below the posted limit if visibility or traction demands it.

Now answer:

What is the MINIMUM following distance on a freeway in good conditions?

Answer: 2 seconds
2-second minimum gap on the freeway in good weather. Double it in rain, triple it in snow or heavy fog. Handbook p. 49.

Quick check

You're on the on-ramp to I-15 and traffic on the interstate is heavy with no clear gap. What do you do?

Answer: Use the acceleration lane to match speed, then merge at a gap. Yield — interstate traffic has priority
On-ramp traffic yields to interstate traffic. Use the acceleration lane to match speed, then merge when safe. Handbook p. 60.
MUST KNOW

City and town speeds: 25 in neighborhoods

In any business or residential area, the speed limit is 25 mph unless posted otherwise.

Even matching the flow of other cars doesn't make it legal to exceed the posted limit.

Handbook p. 54.  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD KNOW

Highway speeds: 65, 70, 75, or 80 — obey the sign

Utah rural interstates are posted from 65 to 80 mph, with transition zones as you approach cities.

  • The posted limit is the legal ceiling — never above, regardless of traffic flow
  • If you're in the left lane and someone catches up behind you, move safely right

SCENARIO

What's wrong with this picture?

Tasha is borrowing her aunt's car. She slides the seat back to give herself 10 inches of chest space, but now the headrest is hitting her neck. Her 14-month-old nephew is in the back, forward-facing.
Two fixes: (1) Raise the headrest until it touches the back of her head above her ears. (2) A 14-month-old is too young to be forward-facing — rear-facing until at least 2 years and 30 lbs.

Tests: seat-10-inches-from-wheel, headrest-above-ears, rear-facing-2-years-30-lbs.

SCENARIO

Who's actually right at the merge point?

Two lanes of I-15 are merging into one because of construction. Some drivers merged into the right lane early; others stay in the left lane to the cones. A driver behind you honks at someone using the zipper.
The zipper crowd is correct. Use both lanes to the merge, then alternate one-by-one. Drivers who merge early make the line longer for everyone. The honker is wrong.

Tests: zipper-merge, lane-change-procedure.

Quick check

A broken (dashed) line on YOUR side of the yellow center stripe means what?

Answer: Passing is permitted when safe to do so
Broken/dashed line on your side = passing PERMITTED (when it's safe to do so).
SCENARIO

Can Owen park here?

Owen finds a curbside spot downtown that's 18 ft from a fire hydrant and 25 ft from a stop sign. Curb is unmarked.
No. 18 ft from a hydrant is fine (rule is 15 ft minimum). But 25 ft from a stop sign breaks the 30-ft rule. Find another spot.

Tests: no-park-15-ft-hydrant, no-park-30-ft-signal.

SCENARIO

The rain just started on I-15. What changes?

Diego is doing the posted 70 mph on I-15 with a 2-second gap. Heavy rain starts. The car ahead is suddenly hard to see clearly.
Two moves: (1) Increase the following gap — 2 seconds is the minimum for good conditions. Aim for 4 seconds in rain. (2) Slow down. Posted limit is the ceiling, not the target.

Tests: freeway-2-sec-follow, never-exceed-posted-limit.

Quick check

What's the default speed limit in a business or residential area (unless posted otherwise)?

Answer: 25 mph
25 mph in business or residential areas. Handbook p. 54.
SCENARIO

You're at the posted limit in the left lane. Someone wants past.

Diego is cruising in the left lane of I-15 at the posted speed limit. A car pulls up behind him, clearly wanting to pass.
Move safely to the right. The left lane is for passing. Even at the posted limit, blocking traffic in the left lane can get you a citation for impeding traffic. Don't speed up — move over.

Tests: left-lane-must-move-right, left-lane-for-passing.

WEIRD UTAH

The Zipper Is the Law

Utah is one of the few states that codifies the zipper merge. Not zippering — merging early when both lanes were marked for use — is technically a traffic infraction.

Next time someone honks at a "late merger" on I-15, they're honking at the person who's actually following the law. Wild.

Top 5 MUSTs from Unit 2

  1. Everyone in the car gets a seat belt or child seat. Kids 12 and under in the back.
  2. Zipper merge: use both lanes to the merge point, then alternate.
  3. Parking distances: 15 ft hydrant, 20 ft crosswalk, 30 ft stop sign/signal, 50 ft railroad.
  4. 2-second following distance on the freeway. More in rain, fog, or snow.
  5. 25 mph in business or residential areas unless posted otherwise.
