Unit 4: Impairment

Alcohol, drugs, fatigue, distraction — what the rules are and how to stay clear of them.

Handbook pages 61–70 · ~18 minutes  |  Zero Fatalities

What this unit gives you

Four straight answers to four normal questions:

  • What are the alcohol rules at your age?
  • What about prescription or over-the-counter meds?
  • What do you do when you are tired behind the wheel?
  • When can you use your phone in the car?

No scare stats. Just the rules, the consequences, and the move.

MUST

What does BAC even mean?

BAC = Blood Alcohol Concentration — the percentage of alcohol in your blood.

The number on a breathalyzer. Higher number = more impairment of four things:

  • Judgment
  • Vision
  • Color distinction
  • Reaction time

Handbook p. 61 · Fact ID: alcohol-reduces-4-systems  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD

Vision starts slipping at 0.02

For everyone — not a legal limit, just biology.

That is why the Utah limit is set conservatively. The rule is not drink until you feel buzzed. Impairment starts with the first drink.

Handbook p. 61 · Fact ID: vision-impaired-at-0.02  |  Zero Fatalities

MUST WEIRD UTAH

The Utah adult limit is 0.05 — lowest in the U.S.

0.05

Every other state is 0.08. Utah moved to 0.05 in 2018 and stayed there. If you ever take a test in another state, do not get tricked — 0.05 is the Utah answer.

CDL (commercial license) is even stricter: 0.04.

Handbook p. 62 · Fact IDs: adult-bac-limit-0.05, cdl-bac-limit-0.04  |  Zero Fatalities

MUST

Under 21? The limit is zero.

0.00

No I only had a sip exception. No but I am fine exception.

Any measurable alcohol = violation. This is called zero tolerance, and every state has a version of it.

Handbook p. 62 · Fact ID: under-21-zero-tolerance  |  Zero Fatalities

Quick check

At what blood alcohol level (BAC) does vision begin to be impaired for all drivers?

Answer: 0.02
Vision is impacted at just 0.02 BAC -- well below any legal driving limit. This is part of why Utah's adult limit is set at 0.05.
MUST

Not-a-Drop Act — 6-month denial

Under 21 + any measurable alcohol while driving =

6 months

...driving privileges denied. First offense.

Second offense within 10 years = 2 years OR until you turn 21 — whichever is longer.

This applies even if you do not have a license yet. They can deny a future one.

Handbook p. 62 · Fact ID: not-a-drop-6-months-first  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD

Two separate rules — do not mix them up

SituationPenalty
Driving under 21 with any alcohol (Not-a-Drop)6 months first offense
Possessing or drinking under 21 (not driving)1 year first, 2 year second

Both can apply to the same situation. They are different statutes.

Handbook p. 62 · Fact ID: under-21-possession-1-2-year-suspension  |  Zero Fatalities

Quick check

What is the legal BAC limit for drivers under 21 in Utah?

Answer: 0.00 -- zero tolerance
Under 21 = 0.00 BAC. Any measurable alcohol in your system while driving violates Utah's Not-a-Drop / zero-tolerance law.
MUST

First DUI under 21 = 3-year IID

IID = Ignition Interlock Device. A breathalyzer wired to the ignition — the car will not start if it detects alcohol.

3 years

That is the under-21 first-offense IID period. Adults get 18 months for a first DUI — under-21 is longer.

Handbook p. 64 · Fact ID: iid-3-years-under-21-first  |  Zero Fatalities

Quick check

Alcohol and impairing drugs reduce which four driver systems?

Answer: Judgment, vision, color distinction, reaction time
Alcohol and impairing drugs reduce judgment, vision, color distinction, and reaction time -- all four critical to driving.
MUST

Implied consent — refusing the test still costs you the license

By driving in Utah, you have already agreed to take a chemical test (breath, blood, urine, saliva) if an officer asks. That is the Implied Consent Law.

Refuse the test? License revoked.

WhoFirst refusalSecond
21+18 months36 months
Under 212 years36 months OR until 21

Alcohol-restricted status — the tiers

After a DUI conviction, you become alcohol-restricted for a set period:

TierLength
Standard tiers2, 3, 5, or 10 years
Automobile homicide / felony DUILIFETIME

Driving with any measurable alcohol during this period = 1-year revocation on top.

MUST

Drugs means all three categories

Utah DUI law treats impairing drugs the same way regardless of source:

  • Illegal (street drugs)
  • Over-the-counter (cold meds, sleep aids, antihistamines)
  • Prescription (even if the doctor wrote it for you)

If it impairs your driving, it counts. Doctor approval is not a free pass — you are still responsible for whether you can drive safely.

Handbook p. 61 · Fact ID: impairing-drugs-all-three-categories  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD

Read the label. Ask the pharmacist.

May cause drowsiness on a label is a real warning. Cold meds, allergy meds, sleep aids, and many prescriptions can impair driving.

The move: first dose of a new med? Skip driving that day if you can. See how it hits you.

Handbook p. 31, 66 · Fact ID: meds-cause-drowsiness  |  Zero Fatalities

How long is the Alcohol-Restricted Driver status in Utah?

Answer: 2, 3, 5, or 10 years depending on offense
Alcohol-Restricted status lasts 2, 3, 5, or 10 years depending on the offense count and type.

Utah's Implied Consent Law means what?

By driving in Utah, you have already AGREED to submit to a chemical test (breath/blood/urine/oral fluids) if a peace officer asks based on suspicion.

An under-21 driver convicted of a first DUI has an Ignition Interlock requirement of:

Answer: 3 years
Under-21 first DUI = 3-year IID requirement. That is longer than the adult first-DUI period (18 months). Handbook p. 64.
SHOULD

Drowsy driving — the warning signs

Your body sends signals before you nod off. Catch any of these:

  • Cannot remember the last few miles
  • Hit a rumble strip or drifted in your lane
  • Yawning, heavy eyes, wandering thoughts
  • Tailgating without meaning to
  • Missing signs or exits

Handbook p. 67 · Fact ID: drowsy-warning-signs-list  |  Zero Fatalities

MUST

Drowsy? Here is the move.

Pull over. Switch drivers, or take a 20-minute power nap.

On a long trip, plan to stop every 2 hours. Most people need 7–9 hours of sleep to drive their best.

Coffee plus open window does not actually fix sleep debt — it just delays the crash by a few minutes.

Handbook p. 66–67 · Fact IDs: power-nap-20-min, long-trip-stop-every-2-hours  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD

What counts as distraction

Anything that takes your mind or body away from driving.

Not just phones. Also: eating, makeup, fiddling with radio or GPS, reaching for something on the floor, loud passengers, an argument in the car.

The move (before you drive): set GPS, queue music, eat, secure loose stuff. Now you do not need to mid-drive.

Handbook p. 65 · Fact ID: distraction-definition-mind-or-body  |  Zero Fatalities

Quick check

Which of these is a warning sign you are too drowsy to drive?

Answer: Cannot remember the last few miles, drifting in lane, or hitting rumble strips
Drowsy warning signs include: cannot remember the last few miles, drift in lane, hit rumble strip, missing signs, heavy eyes, constant yawning.
MUST

Texting while driving = Class C, $100 max

Texting while operating a moving vehicle is a Class C misdemeanor, max fine $100.

If you cause injury, or you have a prior texting offense: bumps up to Class B misdemeanor.

Officers can pull you over just for texting. It is a primary offense — they do not need another reason.

Quick check

At what point does alcohol impairment begin?

Answer: With the very first drink
Impairment begins with the first drink. You do not have to feel drunk to be impaired enough to drive unsafely.
MUST

Phone rules split at age 18

Under 1818 and over
Phone use allowed only for: medical emergency, safety hazard, criminal activity, or parent/guardian.May talk via voice commands (hands-free). No manual dialing or typing.

So at 16: even hands-free chatting with a friend = not allowed. Parent? Fine.

MUST

The no-manual rule (every age, every driver)

While operating a moving vehicle on a highway, you may NOT manually:

  • Write, send, or read a text / IM / email
  • Dial a number
  • Access the internet
  • Take a photo or record video
  • Enter data into a wireless device

Voice command around all of those? Legal (if you are 18+).

Handbook p. 67 · Fact ID: hwc-general-no-manual  |  Zero Fatalities

SHOULD

Passengers count too

The GDL (Graduated Driver License) limits passengers under 21 for a reason — loud car, friends being friends, your attention splits.

The move: if the car gets chaotic, pull over and reset. Quiet down or I pull over is a real driver tool.

Handbook p. 65 · Fact ID: distraction-passengers  |  Zero Fatalities

MUST

If alcohol is in the picture — the plan

Decide before, not after.

  • Designated driver who did not drink
  • Rideshare (Uber, Lyft)
  • Sleep over — stay where you are
  • Call a parent. Seriously. Come get me beats every other option.

None of these depend on you doing math on how impaired you feel.

Empowerment framing · Fact ID: pre-drive-distraction-plan

Quick check

A driver UNDER 18 may use a wireless device while driving ONLY for which purposes?

Answer: Medical emergency, safety hazard, criminal activity, OR contacting parent/guardian
Under 18 may only use a wireless device for medical emergency, safety hazard, criminal activity, OR contacting parent/legal guardian.
SCENARIO

Scenario: One sip under 21

Jordan, 18, has a sip of beer at a friend house. Drives home. Officer pulls him over for a broken taillight, smells alcohol.

The result: Not-a-Drop Act — 6-month license denial. Possession statute — 1-year suspension on top. Only a sip is not a defense; under 21 means zero.

Tests: under-21-zero-tolerance, not-a-drop-6-months-first

SCENARIO

Scenario: Refusing the breathalyzer

Andre, 22, gets pulled over, suspected DUI. He refuses the breathalyzer thinking it will protect him.

The result: Implied Consent kicks in — 18-month automatic revocation for first refusal, separate from any DUI charges. The refusal itself is the violation.

Tests: implied-consent-law, refusal-21-plus-18-or-36-months

SCENARIO

Scenario: Cold medicine + driving

Brian takes a nighttime cold med (sleep aid included) two hours before driving to a friend house.

The result: OTC meds count. Sleep aid plus driving = impaired. If a test shows the metabolite after a crash, that is a DUI charge — not just a civil ticket. Read the label, swap to non-drowsy, or do not drive.

Tests: impairing-drugs-all-three-categories, meds-cause-drowsiness

Quick check

Which categories of drugs are considered impairing drugs under Utah law?

Answer: Illegal, over-the-counter, AND prescription drugs
Impairing drugs include all three: illegal, over-the-counter, AND prescription. A doctor note does NOT exempt you from DUI law.
SCENARIO

Scenario: Texting at a red light

Sam, 16, stopped at a red light. Pulls out his phone to text a friend running 5 min late. Patrol officer in the next lane sees him.

The result: Under 18 + texting a friend is not one of the four exceptions (medical, hazard, criminal, parent/guardian). Class C misdemeanor, max $100 fine. Stopped at a light still counts as operating a moving vehicle in traffic.

Tests: hwc-under-18-exceptions-only, texting-class-c-100

SCENARIO

Scenario: Hands-free at 22

Marisa, 22, uses voice command to dial her mom on the freeway. Talks hands-free for 5 minutes.

The result: Legal. Over 18 may talk via voice commands. No manual dialing happened. Whether the call is mom, GPS, or general chat does not matter at 18+ — only the manual rule applies.

Tests: hwc-over-18-voice-only

MUST

Flooded road? Turn around.

Water depthWhat happens
6 inchesStalls most passenger cars
2 feetSweeps away most vehicles — including SUVs and trucks

You cannot see what is under the water. The road might be washed out. The water might be moving faster than it looks.

The move: Turn around. Find another route. Turn Around, Do Not Drown is the literal slogan.
