Safety belts

  • Everyone in the vehicle must have a fastened safety belt or child seat.
  • Air bags work WITH belts, not as a replacement.
  • Lap belt across hips, NOT stomach. Shoulder belt across shoulder/chest, NOT under arm.

Kids in the back

  • Children 12 and younger should ride in the back seat.
  • Rear-facing until at least 2 yrs + 30 lbs.
  • Forward-facing harness until at least 4 yrs + 40 lbs.
  • Booster until belt fits + child is 57 inches tall.
  • Infants NEVER ride in the front seat with a passenger-side air bag.

Bicyclists

  • Pass with at least 3 feet of distance (minimum, by law).
  • Bicycles are vehicles on roadways — same rights and responsibilities as cars.
  • Bike lanes are marked with solid white lines.

Pedestrians (you, when walking)

  • No sidewalk? Walk on the LEFT shoulder, facing oncoming traffic.

Big trucks (semis)

  • A fully loaded tractor-trailer at 55 mph may take over 400 feet to stop. About 1.5× a football field.
  • If you can't see the truck's mirrors, the driver can't see you.
  • Don't cut in front: they can't brake like a car.

Headlights

  • ON from sunset to sunrise, OR whenever you can't clearly see 1,000 ft ahead.
  • Dim high beams within 500 ft of oncoming vehicle.
  • Dim high beams within 300 ft when following another vehicle.
