Who needs a license

  • Every Utah resident driving on public highways must have a valid Utah DL, learner permit, or temporary DL.
  • Cannot have a Utah DL if your driving privilege is denied / suspended / revoked / disqualified in Utah OR any other state.

Learner permit (age 15)

  • Min age 15 for entry-level learner permit.
  • Must have parent / guardian / instructor OR licensed 21+ adult (with authorization) in the front seat.
  • Hold permit 6 months if age 16–17.

Driver ed + practice

  • Under 18: hold permit 6 months AND complete approved driver ed.
  • Driver ed requires 40 hrs practice driving, including 10 hrs after sunset with parent/guardian.
  • Age 19+ with no prior license: can skip driver ed if permit held 90 days + 40 hours practice.

License types

  • Class D = regular DL for personal vehicles up to 26,000 lbs.
  • Anyone under 21 with Class D gets a Provisional license (separate point system).

Endorsement letters

  • M = Motorcycle (required to ride one on Utah highways).
  • H = Hazmat.

The tests

  • Written knowledge test: pass at 80%; untimed; 30–45 min typical.
  • Traffic Safety & Trends Exam: online only, must score 100%, required for first-time Class D applicants.
  • Skills test: bring your own vehicle.
  • Third-Party Testers do skills test ONLY — not the written knowledge test.

Vision

  • Standard: 20/40 + 90° peripheral field in at least one eye.

16-year-old extra rules

  • No driving 12:00 a.m. – 5:00 a.m. (school/work/emergency/medical exceptions).
  • First 6 months after license: immediate family only in the car.

License expiration

  • Provisional (under 21): expires 5 years from issue, on birthday.
  • Regular (21+): expires 8 years from issue, on birthday.
